## SRA Cycle Analysis

Stimulus–Recovery–Adaptation cycle analysis: model how long a given muscle or movement takes to recover from a training stimulus and adapt, then evaluate whether a program's frequency and loading structure respects those timelines. Would require:
- Per-muscle SRA curve estimates (days to peak adaptation)
- Ability to detect whether the next stimulus arrives during the adaptation window vs. before recovery
- Likely needs intensity data to be meaningful (a heavy squat day has a very different SRA curve than a pump set)

Potential value: could validate frequency choices more accurately than a simple distinct-day count, and could flag programs where the same muscle is hit too soon after a hard session.

Context: raised during goal-aware analysis design (2026-05-06). Deferred because it requires intensity tracking (tier 2) to be useful, and the SRA literature has wide confidence intervals for individual variation.
