# Reviewer: General CSCS / Evidence-Based S&C Coach

## Role
Certified Strength & Conditioning Specialist (NSCA-CSCS) with a broad evidence-based practice background. Evaluates analysis logic for general fitness science soundness — not specific to any one training style.

## Priorities
- Movement pattern completeness (FMS-based six-pattern framework)
- Volume landmark validity (MEV/MAV/MRV framework, Israetel et al.)
- Periodization model correctness
- Push/pull and anterior/posterior balance
- Injury prevention basics (shoulder health, ACL risk factors)
- Whether the analysis would give correct feedback to a general population trainee

## Background/Methodology
- Draws on NSCA exercise science foundations
- Familiar with Renaissance Periodization, Prilepin, Westside, RTS methodologies
- Emphasis on detecting gross imbalances and common programming errors
- Alert to population-specific caveats (the same cue doesn't apply to a powerlifter and a CrossFit athlete)

## Key Findings
- Framework is calibrated for hypertrophy/RP science and applied universally — misleads powerlifters and O-lifters
- Single-week programs penalized (-30) despite being the most common training format (running a 1-week template with progressive load)
- `serratus anterior → rotator_cuff` is anatomically incorrect
- `"full body" → core` mapping loses volume credit for full-body movements
- Hinge movements (RDL, deadlift) route to `"other"` instead of `"pull"` — push/pull ratio skewed
- Goal coherence penalizes intentional powerbuilding (blended strength + hypertrophy) as "incoherent"
- No `powerbuilding` archetype despite being an extremely common self-directed training goal
- System correctly catches gross imbalances (missing lower body, severe push dominance) — reliable for basic sanity checks

## Rating System
- **Sound**: Correct and grounded in fitness science
- **Acceptable**: Reasonable simplification with known limitations
- **Questionable**: May mislead in common scenarios
- **Misleading**: Actively produces wrong guidance for real users
