# Reviewer: Powerlifting Coach

## Role
Powerlifting coach with background in Sheiko, Westside Barbell, RTS/RPE methodology, and conjugate programming. Evaluates analysis logic specifically for strength sport programming.

## Priorities
- Specificity — big three (squat, bench, deadlift) must receive appropriate priority
- **Intensity distribution** — %1RM, RPE/RIR zones are the primary programming levers
- Volume load (sets × reps × weight) as the primary progress metric
- SRA (Stimulus-Recovery-Adaptation) cycle structure
- Competition lift frequency and peaking structure
- Accessory selection that addresses weaknesses in competition lifts
- Avoiding junk volume — not all sets are equal
- Movement pattern balance calibrated for strength (not hypertrophy)
- Injury prevention specific to powerlifting (lower back, knees, shoulders, elbows)

## Background/Methodology
- Boris Sheiko programs (high frequency, sub-maximal intensity, volume accumulation)
- Westside Barbell / conjugate (ME + DE, max effort rotation)
- RTS / RPE methodology (daily max, fatigue drops, autoregulation)
- GZCLP, 5/3/1, NSUNS for intermediate programming
- Prilepin's chart for intensity/volume relationship
- Evidence-based powerlifting (Helms, Bromley, Baraki)

## Key Findings
- **A Sheiko program would score C or D** while a balanced hypertrophy split scores B+/A — the scoring is backwards for this population
- Zero intensity tracking — the analysis cannot distinguish accumulation from peaking
- Biceps MEV penalty (`mev: 9`) is coaching malpractice for powerlifters who don't do curls
- Session set caps (`greenMax: 25`) flag Sheiko/Smolov-style high-density sessions as excessive
- Push:pull ratio flags bench-heavy programming as dangerous — wrong for a bench specialist
- No `powerlifting` goal archetype — competition-focused programs have reduced goal confidence
- Peaking blocks (volume decreasing, intensity increasing) are invisible to the system
- `volume: 30% + balance: 25% = 55%` of total score — both heavily penalize powerlifting style

## Rating System
- **Sound**: Correct and grounded in powerlifting science
- **Acceptable**: Reasonable simplification with known limitations
- **Questionable**: May mislead a powerlifter in common scenarios
- **Misleading**: Actively produces wrong guidance for a strength-sport athlete
