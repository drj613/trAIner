# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

One person, one device. The user is someone who trains seriously and already thinks about programming — they get routines from a human trainer or an external LLM and want somewhere real to run them. Two contexts matter. At home or on the couch they import a pasted program, resolve exercises, edit sets and reps, and read the analysis. In the gym, mid-set, they log reps and weight fast on a phone. There are no accounts and no second user — this is a private tool, not a shared or social one.

## Product Purpose

trAIner turns routines from human trainers or external LLMs into structured, editable, loggable workouts that keep working offline after install. It exists so a serious trainee has a private place to run and record their training without handing their data to anyone. Everything — programs, logs, settings — lives in the browser's IndexedDB; nothing is sent to a server. Success is frictionless import→train: the user goes from a pasted program to logging today's session with no fuss, offline, and keeps coming back because the log is fast and trustworthy.

## Positioning

A private, local-first training log — serious tracking and routine analysis that lives entirely on your device, no cloud, no telemetry, yours to keep. The app isn't selling anything; it earns trust by feeling like a well-built instrument the user owns.

## Brand Personality

Precise and instrument-like, calm, and quietly confident. The interface reads like a well-made tool: exact numbers, tight rhythm, monospace where figures matter, nothing decorative for its own sake. It stays low-stimulation and gets out of the way while the user trains and logs. It speaks to someone who trains seriously and doesn't over-explain or condescend. Its existing IDE- and terminal-tinged aesthetic is the right register, not an accident.

## Anti-references

Not loud fitness-app hype — no neon gradients, motivational-poster energy, gamified confetti, or hustle-culture tone. Not a generic SaaS dashboard of rounded card grids, pastel gradient hero-metrics, and the purple-blue startup template look. Not a consumer social/feed app — no avatars, likes, streak-shaming, or engagement bait. And not a sterile clinical medical UI — cold, gray, and joyless is as wrong as loud.

## Design Principles

The data is the interface. Numbers, sets, and analysis carry the screen; chrome stays minimal and earns its space.

Quiet by default. Low-stimulation, high-density, no motion or color that isn't doing a job. Calm is a feature for something used mid-workout.

Instrument, not toy. Every control is exact and predictable. Trust comes from precision, not persuasion — the app never hypes or gamifies.

Local-first is the product, not a disclaimer. Ownership, export/backup, and offline reliability are treated as first-class, visible affordances.

No friction between intent and logging. The path from a pasted program to a logged set stays as short as physically possible, on phone and desktop alike.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No specific external requirements. Follow sensible defaults: readable contrast across every theme, keyboard and touch both viable, and reduced-motion respected since motion is only ever enhancement here.
